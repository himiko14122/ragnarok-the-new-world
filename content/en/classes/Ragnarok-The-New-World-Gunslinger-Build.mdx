## Gunslinger Build Guide

The Gunslinger is the most versatile DPS class in Ragnarok The New World, distinguished by its unique weapon swap mechanic that allows mid-combat switching between ranged and melee weapons. This flexibility eliminates the situational weaknesses that plague other DPS classes, enabling the Gunslinger to maintain optimal damage output regardless of enemy positioning, range requirements, or encounter phase. Mastering the weapon swap system transforms the Gunslinger from a competent damage dealer into an adaptable combat specialist that excels in every content type.

Unlike the other seven classes, the Gunslinger does not have a second job advancement. Instead, the class gains power through its expanding skill tree and the weapon swap mechanic itself, which becomes more impactful as you acquire additional weapon types and learn to swap between them fluidly. This design makes the Gunslinger's progression path distinct from other classes and requires a different approach to build optimization.

## Stat Distribution

### PvE Damage Build

The PvE Gunslinger build maximizes damage output for farming and dungeon content. The stat distribution emphasizes DEX for ranged accuracy and damage, AGI for attack speed, and moderate VIT for survivability during close-range weapon phases.

| Stat | Value | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| STR | 20-30 | Melee weapon damage, carry capacity |
| AGI | 70-90 | ASPD for both weapon types |
| VIT | 30-50 | Survivability during melee phases |
| INT | 1 | No magic damage dependency |
| DEX | 80-100 | Ranged accuracy and damage |
| LUK | 20-40 | Crit supplement |

The AGI investment deserves emphasis because attack speed benefits both weapon types. A high ASPD Gunslinger delivers more hits per second whether using a ranged firearm or a melee blade, making AGI the most universally valuable stat for the class. The 193 ASPD cap is achievable with AGI investment, ASPD food stacking, and equipment bonuses, creating a Gunslinger that fires or strikes with remarkable speed.

### PvP Burst Build

PvP Gunslinger builds shift toward burst damage and evasion rather than sustained output. Higher VIT investment improves survivability against burst combos, while DEX remains critical for hitting evasive targets.

| Stat | Value | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| STR | 30-40 | Melee burst damage |
| AGI | 50-70 | Moderate ASPD with flee |
| VIT | 50-70 | PvP survivability |
| INT | 1 | No benefit |
| DEX | 90-100 | Accuracy against high-flee targets |
| LUK | 1-10 | Minimal investment |

## Skill Build

### Core Skills (Max Priority)

- Rapid Shower: Multi-hit ranged burst, primary ranged damage skill. Each shot deals weapon damage with a chance for critical hits. Max this first for ranged DPS.
- Desperado: Point-blank AoE dealing damage to all nearby targets. Essential for mob farming and close-range PvP encounters. Works with both weapon types when swapped.
- Tracking: Charged long-range single-target attack with high damage coefficient. Used during MVP vulnerability windows for maximum burst contribution.

### Utility Skills

- Adjustment: Increases flee rate, providing defensive utility during weapon swap transitions when you may be exposed to damage.
- Weapon Swap: The defining class mechanic. Practice swapping between your primary ranged weapon and melee backup fluidly. The swap animation is brief but leaves a small vulnerability window.
- Chain Action: Chance to fire additional shots on basic attacks, synergizing with high ASPD builds.

### Situational Skills

- Gatling Fever: Increases ASPD at the cost of movement speed. Use during stationary farming or when a Knight is tanking and you need maximum DPS.
- Snake Eye: Increases hit rate, useful against high-flee targets in PvP and certain MVPs.

## Weapon Swap Mechanics

### Understanding the Swap System

The weapon swap mechanic allows the Gunslinger to switch between two weapon types during combat. The swap is performed through a hotkey or skill activation and takes approximately 0.5 seconds, during which the Gunslinger cannot attack but can still move. The swapped weapon retains its own refinement level, card slots, and element, meaning each weapon must be independently optimized.

### Optimal Weapon Pairs

| Primary Weapon | Secondary Weapon | Use Case |
|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Rifle (Ranged) | Dagger (Melee) | General PvE farming |
| Shotgun (Ranged) | Sword (Melee) | MVP burst phases |
| Pistol (Ranged) | Katana (Melee) | PvP kiting and burst |

The Rifle provides consistent ranged damage for general farming, while the Dagger handles close-quarters situations when enemies close distance unexpectedly. For MVP encounters, the Shotgun delivers higher burst per shot during vulnerability windows, and the Sword provides melee damage when the MVP enters melee-range attack phases.

### Swap Timing

Effective weapon swapping requires recognizing when each weapon type is optimal. Swap to ranged when the target moves away, when AoE danger zones require distance, or when chasing a fleeing opponent in PvP. Swap to melee when the target is stationary within close range, when you need higher burst per second at point-blank, or when ammunition is running low.

## Equipment Priority

### Refinement Order

Since the Gunslinger maintains two weapons, refinement investment doubles compared to single-weapon classes. Prioritize refining your primary weapon first, as it receives the most use. A +7 primary weapon with a +0 secondary outperforms two +3 weapons in most scenarios.

1. Primary weapon to +7
2. Primary weapon to +10
3. Secondary weapon to +7
4. Primary weapon to +12
5. Both weapons to +10

### Card Selection

Weapon cards should complement each weapon's role. Your ranged weapon benefits from cards that increase ranged damage percentage or add elemental damage. Your melee weapon benefits from cards that increase critical rate or add size-dependent damage bonuses.

For budget-conscious players, focus on a single element card for each weapon rather than chasing expensive multi-effect cards. The [safe refining guide](/refining/safe-refining-guide) provides cost projections for reaching your target refinement levels.

## FAQ

### Is the Gunslinger worth the double weapon investment?

The Gunslinger's weapon swap flexibility provides unique advantages that justify the extra investment for players who value adaptability. However, F2P players should consider whether maintaining two weapons fits their budget. The [F2P tier list](/tier-list/f2p-tier-list) ranks the Gunslinger lower specifically because of this cost consideration.

### How do I practice weapon swapping?

Practice weapon swapping on low-level mobs in fields around Prontera. Swap between weapons repeatedly until the motion feels natural and the timing becomes muscle memory. Start with simple swap patterns, then progress to swapping mid-combo as your comfort increases. The swap timing becomes second nature after a few hours of practice.

### What is the Gunslinger's role in MVP hunting?

In MVP hunting, the Gunslinger serves as adaptive DPS, switching between ranged attacks during dangerous AoE phases and melee burst during vulnerability windows. The ability to maintain damage output regardless of range makes the Gunslinger uniquely consistent across all MVP encounter phases. See our [MVP hunting guide](/mvp-hunting/mvp-hunting-guide) for party composition details.

### Does the Gunslinger need second job advancement?

No, the Gunslinger does not have a second job advancement. The class gains power through its skill tree progression and the weapon swap mechanic. This means the Gunslinger reaches its full potential earlier than classes that require second job advancement, but has a lower ceiling than classes that gain significant power spikes from advancement.

### Which element should my weapon cards provide?

Match your weapon element to the content you primarily run. For general farming, a neutral or holy element card provides broad coverage. For specific MVPs, match the element to the MVP's weakness. The element weakness chart in our [MVP weakness guide](/mvp-hunting/mvp-weakness-chart) helps you select optimal element cards for each encounter.

## Refine Cost Planner for Ragnarok The New World

Planning your refinement investment requires understanding the expected costs, material requirements, and time needed to reach your target levels. The safe refining system eliminates the risk of equipment destruction, but the escalating costs at higher levels mean that reaching +15 requires significant financial planning. This cost planner provides the data and formulas needed to budget your refinement journey, whether you are aiming for a practical +7 or the aspirational +15.

The key insight for refinement budgeting is that expected cost can be calculated mathematically. Since the safe system prevents equipment loss, each attempt either succeeds (moving up one level) or fails (staying at the current level). The expected number of attempts to move from level N to level N+1 equals 1 divided by the success rate at level N. Summing these expected attempts across all levels gives the total expected cost for any target refinement level.

## Cost Calculation Method

### Expected Attempts Formula

The expected number of attempts to advance from level N to N+1 is calculated as:

Expected Attempts = 1 / Success Rate at Level N

For example, advancing from +6 to +7 has a 55% success rate, meaning the expected attempts are 1 / 0.55 = 1.82 attempts. Each attempt costs Zeny and materials, so the total expected cost for this single level advancement equals 1.82 multiplied by the per-attempt cost.

### Cumulative Cost Calculation

To calculate the total expected cost to reach a target level, sum the expected costs for each individual level advancement from +0 to your target.

Total Cost = Sum of (Expected Attempts x Per-Attempt Cost) for each level

For higher refinement levels, begin stockpiling rare materials well before you plan to refine. The bottleneck at +10+ is typically the premium catalyst materials rather than Zeny. ## FAQ

### How do I calculate the expected cost for my specific target?

Use the formula: Total Cost = Sum of (1 / Success Rate at Level N) x (Per-Attempt Cost at Level N) for each level from your current level to your target. The tables in this guide provide the values needed for this calculation. Alternatively, use the cumulative cost column to look up your target directly.

### What if my actual costs exceed the expected costs?

The expected costs are statistical averages. Individual results vary based on luck, and some players will spend more than the expected amount while others spend less. Budget approximately 20-30% above the expected cost to account for unfavorable luck. The safe system ensures you never lose equipment, so additional cost only delays your timeline, not your equipment.

### Should I use Kafra VIP for refinement?

VIP refinement bonuses are worth purchasing if you are targeting +10 or higher. The savings at these levels significantly exceed the VIP cost. For +7 and below, the savings are too small to justify VIP purchase for refinement purposes alone. The [safe refining guide](/refining/safe-refining-guide) discusses the VIP system in more detail.

### How do I plan refinement across multiple equipment pieces?

Prioritize weapon refinement first, then allocate remaining budget to armor and accessories. Once your weapon reaches your primary target, shift refinement spending to armor for survivability. The [best gear priority guide](/refining/best-gear-priority) provides a complete multi-piece refinement planning framework.

### Can I reduce refinement costs through events?

Yes, limited-time events sometimes provide refinement cost reduction bonuses, material discounts, or increased success rates. Plan major refinement pushes around these events to maximize cost efficiency. Check the event calendar and community announcements for upcoming refinement events before committing large Zeny expenditures.

## Progression Guide for Ragnarok The New World

Progression in Ragnarok The New World spans multiple parallel tracks that develop simultaneously: character level, job level, equipment refinement, skill development, card collection, and economic resources. Understanding the optimal priority order for these progression tracks prevents wasted resources and ensures each investment compounds toward your next milestone. This guide maps the complete progression journey from character creation to endgame content mastery, with clear priorities at each stage.

The most common progression mistake is spreading resources across multiple tracks instead of concentrating on the highest-return investments. A player who refines their weapon to +7 before touching armor will progress faster than a player who refines everything to +3 simultaneously. Similarly, a player who completes second job advancement before investing heavily in equipment will unlock more powerful skills that accelerate all subsequent content. This guide provides the exact priority order for every progression decision.

## Early Progression Milestones

### Level 1-20: Foundation

Your first milestone is completing the tutorial and initial story quests. During this phase, focus exclusively on following quest markers and learning the game interface. Do not spend Zeny on equipment upgrades, as the quest rewards provide adequate gear for this level range. Save every Zeny you earn for the more impactful investments coming in later phases.

Key unlocks during this phase include the Kafra service system, the skill bar configuration, the stall trading interface, and the basic crafting system. Each of these unlocks expands your gameplay options and should be understood before moving forward. The [getting started guide](/guides/getting-started) covers these initial systems in detail.

### Level 20-40: First Power Spike

The primary milestone during this range is accumulating enough job experience for second job advancement at job level 40. Second job advancement is the single largest power increase in the game, unlocking defining skills like Bowling Bash, Meteor Storm, and ADL. These skills transform your gameplay and dramatically increase your farming and combat efficiency.

Resource priority during this phase remains on reaching the second job threshold. Use [experience boosters](/guides/leveling-guide) from Adventure Coins during grinding sessions. Do not invest heavily in equipment refinement yet, as your pre-advancement gear will be replaced by stronger options after advancing.

## Mid-Progression Milestones

### Second Job Advancement

Upon reaching job level 40, immediately complete the second job advancement quest for your class. This quest involves a series of class-specific trials that test your understanding of your role. The advancement rewards are worth more than any equipment investment at this stage, as your new skills multiply your effective power.

After advancing, reallocate your skill points according to your second job build. Core skills should be maxed first, with utility skills receiving remaining points. The [class builds section](/classes/all-classes-builds) provides optimal skill point allocations for each second job.

### Weapon Refinement to +7

With second job skills unlocked, your next major milestone is weapon refinement to +7. The safe refinement system ensures your weapon does not break, but costs increase progressively. Each refinement level provides a damage increase that compounds with your new skills. A +7 weapon with second job skills provides the damage foundation needed for efficient dungeon clearing and MVP contribution.

Allocate your Zeny to weapon refinement before any other equipment upgrades. The damage increase from weapon refinement accelerates all content, including the farming that generates resources for further investment. Check the [safe refining guide](/refining/safe-refining-guide) for cost projections and material requirements at each level.

### Card Collection Priority

The card system provides powerful stat bonuses when slotted into equipment. Early card priorities should focus on damage-increasing cards for your weapon slots. Cards that provide percentage-based damage bonuses scale better than flat stat increases as your base damage grows.

### Mid-Game Progression Checklist

- Second job advancement completed
- Weapon refined to +7 minimum
- Core skills maxed
- First damage card slotted
- Daily dungeon routine established
- Stall trading income generating passive Zeny
- Guild joined for MVP hunting access

## Late Progression Milestones

### Equipment Set Completion

Endgame progression focuses on assembling a complete equipment set with optimal cards and refinement levels. Each equipment slot has best-in-slot options that come from specific content sources: MVP drops, dungeon rewards, crafting recipes, and auction house purchases. Target specific items rather than randomly upgrading whatever drops first.

### Refinement to +10 and Beyond

The +10 refinement milestone represents a significant power increase that separates mid-game from endgame players. Reaching +10 requires substantial Zeny investment and patience through the safe refinement system. Beyond +10, each level provides diminishing returns relative to cost, making the +10 to +12 range the practical target for most players.

### Card Optimization

Endgame card setups involve matching cards to specific content types. An MVP hunting card loadout emphasizes damage and survivability against bosses, while a PvP loadout prioritizes status resistance and burst damage. The [refinement cost planner](/refining/refine-cost-planner) helps budget for the total investment needed across multiple equipment pieces.

## Progression Priority Order

### Resource Allocation Hierarchy

1. Second job advancement (unlocks core skills)
2. Weapon refinement to +7 (highest damage return)
3. Damage cards for weapon slots (percentage bonuses)
4. Daily dungeon equipment upgrades (consistent improvement)
5. Armor refinement to +4 (survivability baseline)
6. Accessory optimization (utility and stat bonuses)
7. Weapon refinement to +10 (endgame damage)
8. Full card optimization per content type
9. Armor refinement to +7 (endgame survivability)

Following this priority order ensures each Zeny investment provides maximum impact on your progression. Deviating from this order, such as refining armor before weapon, results in slower overall advancement because damage increases accelerate resource generation.

## Multi-Class Progression

When maintaining multiple classes through Job Freedom, each class requires its own progression investment. The recommended approach is to fully progress one class before beginning investment in a second class. A single +7 weapon on your main class provides more value than two +4 weapons across two classes.

Once your primary class reaches the late progression stage with a +10 weapon and optimized cards, redirect resources to developing your secondary class. This staggered approach prevents resource dilution and ensures each class receives sufficient investment to perform effectively.

## FAQ

### What should I prioritize: refinement or cards?

Weapon refinement provides more consistent damage improvement than cards early on, making it the first priority. Once your weapon reaches +7, begin investing in damage cards that provide percentage bonuses. Cards scale better at higher refinement levels because their percentage bonuses apply to a larger base damage value.

### How do I know when I am ready for endgame content?

You are ready for endgame MVP hunting and challenging dungeons when your weapon reaches +10, your core skills are maxed, and you have at least basic damage cards slotted. Survivability requirements vary by content, but +4 armor refinement provides a baseline for most encounters. Join [MVP hunting parties](/mvp-hunting/mvp-hunting-guide) even before reaching these milestones to learn encounter mechanics.

### Can I progress as a free-to-play player?

Yes, F2P progression follows the same priority order but at a slower pace due to limited Zeny generation. The safe refinement system ensures F2P players never lose equipment to breakage, and the [F2P guide](/guides/f2p-guide) provides specific strategies for maximizing limited resources. F2P players typically reach endgame milestones two to three times slower than spending players.

### What is the most important milestone?

Second job advancement is the single most impactful milestone. The skills unlocked at second job fundamentally change your class and multiply your effectiveness across all content. Everything before second job is preparation; everything after is leveraging your new power for accelerated progression.

### How does the progression path differ between classes?

The priority order remains consistent across classes, but the specific skill point allocations and equipment targets differ. A Knight prioritizes VIT and defense earlier than a Hunter, while a Mage needs less defensive investment due to Fire Wall crowd control. See the [class builds section](/classes/all-classes-builds) for class-specific progression details.

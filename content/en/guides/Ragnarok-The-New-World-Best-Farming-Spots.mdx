## Best Farming Spots in Ragnarok The New World

Finding the right farming spot determines your Zeny income, experience gain, and rare drop acquisition rate. Ragnarok The New World features a vast open world with diverse mob populations across Midgard, but not all locations provide equal value for your time. This guide identifies the best farming spots organized by level range, resource type, and class compatibility, ensuring you always grind at the most efficient location for your current goals and capabilities.

Farming efficiency depends on three factors: mob density, respawn rate, and drop value. A location with high mob density and fast respawn lets you maintain constant combat without downtime, maximizing experience and basic drop income. A location with slower spawns but higher-value drops may provide better Zeny per hour despite lower kill volume. The optimal farming spot depends on whether you prioritize experience, Zeny, or specific rare drops.

## Farming Spots by Level Range

### Level 10-30: Izlude Surroundings

The fields immediately surrounding Izlude provide the first productive farming areas. Poring fields south of Izlude offer dense, low-threat mob spawns that are ideal for early job leveling. The experience per kill is modest, but the high kill rate and minimal downtime compensate with consistent output.

| Location | Mob Types | Drop Value | Best Class |
|----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| South Izlude Fields | Poring, Fabre | Low | Any |
| Izlude East Gate | Lunatic, Chonchon | Low-Medium | Mage |
| Coastal Path | Roda Frog, Drops | Low | Swordsman |

At these levels, prioritize following the main story quests rather than dedicated farming. The quest rewards provide better total value than grinding these early areas. Save dedicated farming sessions for later levels where the returns per hour increase substantially.

### Level 30-50: Prontera Region

The Prontera region opens up significantly more profitable farming opportunities. Mob density increases, drop tables improve, and the variety of mob types allows class-specific optimization.

| Location | Mob Types | Drop Value | Best Class |
|----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Prontera South Gate | Soldier Skeleton, Archer Skeleton | Medium | Knight (Bowling Bash) |
| Prontera Fields West | pecopeco, Muka | Medium | Mage (Fire Wall) |
| Geffen Outskirts | Poison Spore, Venomous | Medium-High | Hunter (ADL) |
| Payon Cave Entrance | Zombie, Skeleton | Medium | Mage (AoE) |

The Prontera South Gate area is particularly valuable for Knights using Bowling Bash, as the skeleton mobs cluster naturally and respawn rapidly. The Mage benefits from Geffen area mobs that funnel well through Fire Wall kill zones. For detailed progression strategies at these levels, see our [leveling guide](/guides/leveling-guide).

### Level 50-70: Advanced Zones

Advanced farming zones provide the highest base income through both mob drops and rare material acquisition. These zones require more capable equipment and higher skill levels to farm efficiently.

Endgame farming focuses on specific resource targets rather than general mob grinding. At this stage, your farming activities align with specific needs: MVP materials for crafting, rare card drops for equipment, or pure Zeny generation through high-density mob farming.

## Class-Specific Farming Strategies

### Mage and Wizard Farming

The Mage and Wizard farming strategy revolves around AoE damage and Fire Wall crowd control. The optimal pattern involves luring mobs into a Fire Wall trap, then unleashing Meteor Storm or Storm Gust to eliminate the cluster. This method achieves the highest kill rate per hour of any class, making the Mage the most efficient [Zeny farmer](/zeny-farming/zeny-farming-guide) in the game.

SP management is the primary constraint for Wizard farming. Carry sufficient SP recovery items and plan your farming route around Kafra service points for quick restocking. The SP food stacking strategy from buff foods provides additional SP regeneration during extended sessions.

### Knight and Blacksmith Farming

The Knight and Blacksmith use mob-gathering strategies where you run through a cluster of enemies, then use Bowling Bash or Cart Revolution to hit multiple targets simultaneously. The pattern requires more movement than Mage farming but generates comparable income when executed efficiently.

The Blacksmith's Cart Revolution provides knockback that can cluster mobs for repeated hits, creating a vacuum farming effect. Additionally, the Blacksmith's vending skill allows immediate sale of farmed materials through the stall system, converting drops to Zeny without visiting an NPC vendor.

### Hunter Farming

The Hunter's ADL build excels at sustained single-target damage, making it ideal for farming locations with spread-out mob spawns rather than clustered groups. The Falcon companion adds automatic supplemental damage that requires no input, creating a low-effort farming pattern.

For mob-dense areas, the Trapper build alternative uses land mines and trap skills to create AoE kill zones. While less popular than ADL for general farming, the Trapper build can match Wizard efficiency in specific locations with predictable mob pathing.

## Offline Farming Setup

The auto hunting system allows your character to continue farming while you are offline. Configure auto hunting before logging out by setting your farming location, active skills, and consumable usage parameters. The offline farming rate is approximately 30-40% of active play efficiency, but the passive income adds up significantly during sleep or work hours.

### Auto Hunting Configuration Tips

- Set your auto hunting location to your current optimal farming spot
- Enable skill usage for your primary AoE attack
- Set consumable thresholds to use healing items at 60% HP
- Choose a location where your class can survive without active dodging
- Verify the auto hunting settings each time before logging out

The auto hunting system generates both experience and drop items, though rare drops occur less frequently during auto hunting than during active play. For maximum [Zeny farming efficiency](/zeny-farming/zeny-farming-guide), configure auto hunting in locations with consistent, high-volume basic drops rather than hoping for rare drops.

## FAQ

### What is the best Zeny farming spot overall?

The Clock Tower area and GH Entrance are consistently the best Zeny farming spots for well-geared characters, providing high drop rates and mob density. For less-geared characters, the Prontera South Gate area provides reliable income with lower gear requirements. The [Zeny farming guide](/zeny-farming/zeny-farming-guide) has detailed income estimates for each location.

### How does mob density affect farming efficiency?

Higher mob density increases your kills per minute, which directly translates to more experience, more basic drops, and more chances for rare drops per hour. Classes with strong AoE benefit disproportionately from dense mob areas because their damage scales with the number of targets hit simultaneously.

### Can I farm effectively with auto hunting?

Auto hunting generates meaningful passive income at approximately 30-40% of active play efficiency. It is best used as a supplement to active farming rather than a replacement. Configure auto hunting for overnight periods and during work hours to maximize the passive accumulation that supplements your active farming sessions.

### What farming spot is best for card drops?

MVP encounters provide the most valuable card drops, followed by dungeon boss encounters and elite mob spawns in advanced zones. Field mob card drops are rare but accumulate over thousands of kills. The [MVP hunting guide](/mvp-hunting/mvp-hunting-guide) covers card drop sources in detail.

### Should I farm for experience or Zeny?

In the early and mid-game, farm for experience, as leveling unlocks better farming spots and higher income potential. In the late game, farm for Zeny to invest in refinement and equipment that enable endgame content. The transition point occurs around level 60-70 when further leveling provides diminishing returns compared to gear investment. Check our [progression guide](/guides/progression-guide) for milestone planning.

## Best PvP Classes and Builds

Choosing the right class for PvP in Ragnarok The New World determines your effectiveness in every competitive encounter, from 1v1 arena duels to massive War of Emperium castle sieges. Each class brings distinct strengths to PvP content, and understanding which classes excel in which formats allows you to make informed decisions about where to invest your PvP efforts. This guide ranks every class for PvP, provides optimized skill builds for the top performers, and explains how each class contributes to the major PvP formats.

PvP class effectiveness differs significantly from PvE tier rankings. A class that dominates PvE farming through AoE capability may struggle in PvP where single-target burst, survivability, and crowd control matter more. The [PvE tier list](/tier-list/pve-tier-list) should not guide your PvP class choice. Instead, focus on the specific PvP attributes that determine arena and siege success: burst damage potential, crowd control availability, survivability against focus fire, and utility for team compositions.

## Assassin PvP Build

### Core Burst Build

The Assassin's PvP power comes from Sonic Blow, a multi-hit burst skill that deals devastating damage within a single combo window. The PvP build maximizes Sonic Blow damage and the gap-close capability needed to deliver it.

**Key Skills**:

| Skill | Level | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Sonic Blow | Max | Primary burst damage |
| Cloaking | Max | Stealth approach, gap close |
| Enchant Poison | Max | Damage amplification |
| Grimtooth | 5 | Pre-engagement poke |
| Back Slide | 3 | Disengage after burst |
| Venom Dust | 3 | Area denial during retreat |

**Combo Sequence**: Cloaking to approach undetected, Enchant Poison pre-buff, Sonic Blow on target, Back Slide to disengage if the target survives. If the Sonic Blow kills the target, immediately Cloak again to prepare for the next engagement. The [Thief class guide](/classes/all-classes-builds) covers the full skill progression path.

**1v1 Strategy**: Against ranged classes, use Cloaking to close distance before the opponent can react. Against Knights, bait Provoke cooldown before committing Sonic Blow. Against Priests, time your burst for their healing cooldown window. The Assassin wins 1v1 through one-combo kills, not sustained fighting.

**3v3/WoE Strategy**: In team formats, the Assassin targets the enemy healer first. A Priest kill collapses the enemy's sustain, creating a cascade advantage. Coordinate with your team's CC to guarantee the Sonic Blow lands during a stun or freeze window. The [War of Emperium guide](/pvp/war-of-emperium-guide) covers Assassin surgical strike tactics in siege contexts.

## Knight PvP Build

### Frontline Sustain Build

The Knight's PvP role is absorbing damage, controlling space, and protecting backline teammates through Provoke aggro management. The PvP build maximizes HP, damage reduction, and Provoke effectiveness.

**Key Skills**:

| Skill | Level | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Provoke | Max | Force enemy attention on Knight |
| Bowling Bash | Max | AoE damage for clustered enemies |
| Two-Hand Quicken | Max | ASPD for sustained DPS |
| Endure | 5 | Prevent flinching during push |
| Magnum Break | 3 | Knockback for space control |
| Bash | 5 | Single-target interrupt |

**Positioning Strategy**: In all team formats, the Knight stands between the enemy and the backline. The Knight absorbs enemy burst through high HP and defense while the backline DPS and healers operate safely. Provoke any enemy Assassin that attempts to bypass the frontline, forcing them to waste their burst on the Knight instead of the Priest.

**1v1 Strategy**: The Knight wins through attrition. Survive the initial burst from burst classes, then gradually out-sustain them through higher HP and defense. Against sustained DPS classes like the Hunter, close the gap with Endure preventing flinch and deliver consistent melee damage. The [Knight build guide](/classes/swordsman-knight-build) covers the full PvE-to-PvP skill transition.

The Hunter's PvP strength is consistent ranged damage from a safe position. The PvP build maximizes ADL damage output and trap utility for area control.

| Skill | Level | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Double Strafe | Max | Primary PvP damage skill |

| Claymore Trap | 3 | Area denial |

| Skill | Level | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Storm Gust | Max | AoE damage + freeze CC |
| Fire Wall | Max | Barrier and zone control |
| Meteor Storm | 5 | AoE burst in team fights |

**Arena Strategy**: The Wizard excels in 3v3 and 5v5 where clustered enemies create AoE value. Position behind the Knight frontline and cast Storm Gust through the engagement zone. Use Fire Wall to block corridors and force enemies into disadvantageous positions. In 1v1, the Wizard struggles against gap-closing Assassins and must rely on Frost Diver CC into burst combos.

## FAQ

### Which class should I pick for PvP as a beginner?

The Knight is the most beginner-friendly PvP class because its frontline role is straightforward and forgiving. Stand in front, Provoke enemies, and survive. The Knight's high HP allows mistakes without immediate death, giving new PvP players time to learn mechanics. The Assassin is the strongest PvP class overall but requires precise combo execution that beginners may struggle with initially.

### Can I use my PvE build for PvP?

PvE and PvP builds differ significantly in skill allocation. PvE builds maximize sustained farming output, while PvP builds maximize burst, CC, and survivability. The [Job Freedom system](/guides/job-freedom-guide) allows you to maintain separate builds for each content type. Invest in a dedicated PvP skill allocation for the best results in competitive content.

### Is the Druid viable for PvP?

The Druid provides supplementary healing and nature-based CC, making it a viable flex pick in 3v3 and 5v5. However, the Priest's superior burst healing makes it the preferred primary healer. The Druid works best as a second healer or as a substitute when no Priest is available. The [Druid class guide](/classes/druid-class-guide) covers PvP-focused skill builds.

### How do I counter Assassins in PvP?

Assassins rely on closing distance for Sonic Blow. Counter them by maintaining distance, placing detection traps to reveal Cloak, and saving defensive cooldowns for their burst window. Knights counter Assassins by surviving the burst through high HP. Hunters counter with Ankle Snare traps at approach angles. Priests survive by pre-casting heal before the Sonic Blow lands.

### Does equipment refinement matter in PvP?

Equipment refinement provides a meaningful advantage in PvP, though the arena normalization reduces extreme disparities. A +10 weapon deals noticeably more damage than a +4 weapon even in normalized arenas. For competitive ranked play, invest in [refinement](/refining/safe-refining-guide) up to +7 at minimum. For War of Emperium, where normalization does not apply, equipment differences are even more significant.

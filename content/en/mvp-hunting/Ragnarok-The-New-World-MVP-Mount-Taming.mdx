## MVP Mount Taming Guide

Some of the rarest and most impressive mounts in Ragnarok The New World are obtained through MVP encounters, where specific bosses drop mount items that can be used to tame unique creatures. These MVP mounts offer distinct appearances, superior movement stats, and in some cases, the ability to fly across the open world. This guide covers every MVP that drops mount items, the taming process for each, and strategies for obtaining these prestigious companions.

MVP mount drops are among the rarest items in the game, with drop rates comparable to MVP cards. Obtaining an MVP mount requires either exceptional luck on a single kill or consistent participation in MVP hunting over an extended period. The rarity of these mounts makes them status symbols that demonstrate dedication and commitment to endgame content.

## Taming Process

### Obtaining the Mount Item

When an MVP drops a mount item, it appears in the drop distribution for the eligible party. The mount item is typically a tradeable object until used, meaning it can be sold through the [auction house](/zeny-farming/auction-house-guide) for substantial Zeny. Players who prefer guaranteed acquisition may purchase mount items from other players rather than relying on drop luck.

### Using the Mount Item

To tame an MVP mount, use the mount item from your inventory while in an appropriate open area. The taming process involves a brief animation sequence where your character bonds with the mount creature. Once tamed, the mount is permanently added to your mount collection and can be summoned from any location.

### Mount Requirements

Some MVP mounts have level requirements before they can be used. Flying mounts typically require a higher base level than ground mounts, reflecting their superior capabilities. Ensure you meet the level requirement before purchasing or using a mount item to avoid having a valuable item sitting unused in your inventory.

## Hunting Strategy for Mount Drops

### Target Selection

Focus your [MVP hunting](/mvp-hunting/mvp-hunting-guide) on specific MVPs that drop the mount you want. Rather than hunting random MVPs, track the spawn timers for your target MVP and participate in every kill opportunity. The [MVP spawn timer guide](/mvp-hunting/mvp-spawn-timers) provides complete schedules for all MVPs.

### Kill Volume Strategy

Given the extremely low drop rates for mount items, the most practical acquisition strategy is maximizing your total number of MVP kills. Join an active guild that hunts MVPs consistently, participate in every available kill, and accumulate attempts over weeks and months. The law of large numbers eventually favors consistent participants.

### Economic Acquisition

If you have sufficient Zeny but insufficient time for dedicated MVP hunting, purchasing mount items from the auction house provides a guaranteed path to MVP mounts. The prices reflect the rarity: ground mount items cost millions of Zeny, while flying mount items cost tens of millions. The [Zeny farming guide](/zeny-farming/zeny-farming-guide) covers income strategies for accumulating this level of wealth.

## Mount Usage and Benefits

### Ground Mount Benefits

Ground mounts provide movement speed bonuses that reduce travel time between locations. While the movement speed bonus may seem modest, the cumulative time savings over hundreds of hours of gameplay is significant. Mounts also provide visual prestige, as other players can see which mounts you have collected.

### Flying Mount Benefits

Flying mounts provide the ability to travel through the air, accessing elevated locations and bypassing ground-level obstacles. This capability is transformative for exploration, as many areas of Midgard contain elevated terrain that is inaccessible on foot or ground mount. The [exploration guide](/mounts/exploration-guide) covers locations that require flying mounts.

### Mount in Combat

Mounts provide no combat bonuses in Ragnarok The New World, serving exclusively as transportation and cosmetic items. You cannot attack while mounted and must dismount before engaging in combat. However, the movement speed benefits between combat encounters make mounts valuable for farming efficiency and event participation.

## FAQ

### Can MVP mount items be traded?

Most MVP mount items are tradeable before they are used. Once used to tame the mount, the mount becomes bound to your account and cannot be transferred. Check the specific item description before purchasing to confirm tradeability.

### How rare are flying mount drops?

Flying mount drops from MVPs have estimated drop rates below 0.05% per kill. This means you would need to participate in approximately 2000 or more kills to have a reasonable probability of obtaining one. The extreme rarity makes flying mounts the most prestigious items in the game.

### Are there alternatives to MVP mounts for flying?

Yes, non-MVP flying mounts are available through other systems including quest rewards, event participation, and the standard taming system. These alternative flying mounts have different appearances and slightly lower movement stats than MVP flying mounts but provide the same core flight capability. See the [flying mounts guide](/mounts/flying-mounts-guide) for non-MVP options.

### Can I use mounts across different classes?

Yes, mounts are account-bound and available to all classes through the Job Freedom system. When you switch classes, your mount collection remains accessible. This means investing in a rare mount benefits all your class configurations equally.

### What is the fastest mount in the game?

The MVP flying mounts from Stormy Knight and Dark Lord provide the highest movement speed in the game, both on the ground and in the air. Among ground mounts, the Golden Orc from Orc Lord offers the fastest land movement. The speed difference between mounts is modest, making visual preference a valid selection criterion alongside performance.
